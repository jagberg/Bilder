# Soundproofing Your House

Sound transmission effectiveness is measured using the **Weighted Sound Reduction Index (Rw)**, where higher values indicate better soundproofing. The adjustment factor **Rw+Ctr** accounts for low-frequency noise, which is often the most problematic type.

## Between Floors Solutions

### Hebel Powerfloor

Budget: $15,000–$30,000+

Powerfloors are generally considered the best product (other than a concrete poured floor) for sound insulation. These systems achieve ratings up to 57dB with proper installation, though online reviews are mixed.

### Insulation & Isolation

Sound insulation relies on **density** rather than R-values (which measure thermal performance). Bradford SoundScreen is commonly used for impact noise reduction. Sound isolation clips cost approximately $18/m² and help decouple drywall from structural framing.

## Wall Soundproofing

For airborne noise in walls, dense insulation in cavities is effective. Windows with thicker laminated glass are more effective than double glazing for sound reduction — requiring over 100mm spacing for effective double-glazing performance.

## Technical Measurements

**Structure-borne noise** includes footsteps and dropped objects, measured as LnT,w.

**Airborne noise** (voices, television) requires different mitigation strategies.

The difference between Rw and Rw+Ctr typically ranges from 6–12dB, with Ctr always being negative to account for low-frequency challenges.
