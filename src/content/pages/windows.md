# New Home Windows — Complete Guide

Windows are critical building components that affect aesthetics, airflow, soundproofing, heating, cooling, and privacy. Builders typically provide minimum standard options without discussing available alternatives.

## Material Types

### Timber

- **Pros**: Wide profile options, stainable colours
- **Cons**: High maintenance (sanding, staining, coating), expensive, prone to greying/blackening, difficult to repair from pet damage
- **Best for**: Heritage or aesthetic-focused builds

### Aluminium

- **Standard**: Poor thermal performance without insulation
- **Thermally Broken**: Non-conductive material inserted in frame prevents heat/cold transfer; maintains aluminium appearance
- **Cons**: More maintenance than uPVC

### uPVC

- **Advantages**: Most thermally efficient, wide profiles, low maintenance
- **Cost**: Moderate
- **Best thermal option** overall

### Composite (Timber + Aluminium)

- Inside timber for aesthetics, outside aluminium for durability
- Reduces maintenance significantly compared to pure timber

## Window Classifications

### Residential vs Commercial

- **Residential**: Standard sizes, small frames, typically cannot accommodate double glazing
- **Commercial/Semi-commercial**: Thicker frames, accommodate double glazing, support heights exceeding 2.4m and wider openings
- **Custom sizing**: Costs increase significantly for non-standard dimensions

## Glazing Options

### Double Glazing

- Two panes separated by air pocket, often filled with argon gas
- Reduces thermal loss and provides soundproofing
- Cost: 20–40% more than single glazing (e.g., $500→$700)
- Improves BASIX/NatHERS compliance
- **Weight consideration**: Windows exceeding 80kg may require onsite glazing ($500+) or craning

### Sound/Acoustic Protection

- Double glazing effectiveness varies; requires gaps exceeding 100mm for optimal results
- Laminated glass products like Viridian VLam Hush™ specifically designed for acoustics
- Density is crucial for soundproofing

### Thermal Efficiency Options

- **Low-e glass**: Alternative to double glazing, eliminates need for frame upgrades
- **Comfort glass**: Similar thermal benefits without commercial frame requirements
- Reduces onsite glazing costs

## Thermal Efficiency Metrics

Windows are compared using:

- **U-values**: Measure heat retention (lower is better; ~2.0 is well-performing)
- **SHGC (Solar Heat Gain Coefficient)**: Blocks heat from sunlight (0–1 scale; lower is better)
- **AI (Air Infiltration)**: Seal quality (lower is better; <0.3 is good)

### Frame Impact on Thermal Performance

Thermally broken frames dramatically improve efficiency:

- Standard aluminium + double glazing = U-value ~3.6
- Thermally broken frames achieve U-values ~1.5
- Effect most significant in climates with temperatures below 5°C
- Cost premium: approximately 40% increase

## Skylights

Fixed skylights cost $2,300–$2,700. Velux offers several standard sizes with minimal price differentiation between options.

## Flyscreens

**Typical costs**: $45–$50 per window

**Specialised options**:

- Retractable flyscreens: ~$2,000 for 4m bifold doors
- BAL-rated homes require BAL-certified screens (typically aluminium)
- Casement windows may not accommodate standard flyscreens unless upgraded to full commercial frames

## Key Recommendations

1. **Upgrade frames**: Thermally broken aluminium, timber, or uPVC for improved efficiency
2. **Specify double glazing**: Cost increase justified for thermal/acoustic benefits
3. **Standardise orientations**: Align window headers and heights for visual consistency
4. **Plan for flyscreens**: Provision installation points even if deferring purchase
5. **Use WERS data**: Compare products using standardised U-value and SHGC ratings
6. **Consider total weight**: Factor in onsite glazing costs for windows exceeding 80kg
