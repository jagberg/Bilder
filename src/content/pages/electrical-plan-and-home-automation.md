# Electrical Plan and Home Automation

Electrical planning requires advance thinking to avoid costly modifications later. Consider your daily usage patterns when making decisions.

## Areas to Consider

- **Exterior**: Driveway lighting, porch sensors, entry switches
- **Interior**: Multi-way switches for hallways and stairs, pendant provisions for high ceilings
- **Special needs**: Dimming options for elderly residents, pool circuits, garden lighting
- **Power requirements**: Strategically placed outlets for power tools; three-phase power for high-demand appliances like large air conditioning units

## Lighting

Builders typically include one oyster light per room and basic downlights. Most homeowners prefer extensive downlights (50–100 units), which significantly increases costs.

**Downlight provision** means the hole and electrical socket will be provided and all you have to do is buy the downlight. This budget-friendly option allows homeowners to select premium fixtures themselves.

## Home Automation & Audio

Provisions for smart home systems, speakers, projectors, intercoms, and smart lighting controls add convenience but require careful planning during the electrical stage to avoid expensive retrofitting later.
