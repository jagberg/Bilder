# Lighting Tips and Ideas for a New Home

Good lighting planning is about understanding how spaces will actually be used throughout the day. Poor placement is hard to fix after construction.

## Key Lighting Placement Principles

### Entryways

Downlights should be positioned to illuminate the entry path, with additional fixtures near doors to improve visibility of locks during evening hours.

### Bedrooms

- 2–4 fixtures depending on room size, installed with dimmer capability
- Downlights shouldn't be over the bed but rather to the side of the bed
- A separate light near wardrobes ensures adequate visibility when the door opens

### Bathrooms

- Vanity lighting should be placed above sinks rather than centred in the room to prevent shadows when viewing mirrors
- Shower areas require carefully positioned fixtures to avoid creating harsh shadows

### Kitchens

Multiple lighting types work together:

- Pendant lights over islands and breakfast nooks
- Downlights positioned beside (not behind) appliances like cooktops and refrigerators
- Under-cabinet LED strips for bench illumination
- Dedicated fixtures above sinks to minimise shadows

### Living Areas

Rather than uniform grid patterns, divide circuits strategically — for example, the downlights at the back of the sofa on their own circuit — allowing selective activation for different moods and times of day.

## Technical Specifications

### Downlight Types

- **SMD**: Multiple light sources; more affordable but produces glare
- **COB**: Single light source with reflector; uniform light with less glare

### Colour Rendering Index (CRI)

Higher ratings (90+) render colours more accurately — important for kitchens and work areas.

### Colour Temperature Range

2700–6500K, from warm to daylight options. Warmer tones (2700–3000K) suit bedrooms and living areas; cooler tones (4000–5000K) suit bathrooms and kitchens.

## Future-Proofing Considerations

For homes where lighting modifications may be needed post-handover, install at least one ceiling fixture positioned where multiple lights would eventually go, rather than centred in rooms requiring later patchwork.
