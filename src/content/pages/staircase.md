# Staircase Options for New Home Builds

The staircase is a crucial decision that cannot be easily modified after construction. Builders will generally provide the most basic staircase affordable to them, with even minor upgrades potentially adding $5,000 or more.

## Key Staircase Components

### Stringers

The structural side supports come in three main types:

- **Closed stringers**: Standard option allowing MDF treads and carpet installation
- **Open stringers (sawtooth)**: Adds several thousand dollars; prevents later carpet or laminate changes since balusters interfere
- **Mono stringer**: Steel options with significantly higher costs

### Balusters

Support posts between treads vary in material:

- Steel (most economical, requires no additional finishing)
- Pine (painted, customisable shapes, ~$4 extra per unit)
- Wrought iron (similar pricing to painted pine)
- Glass (approximately $10,000 additional cost)

### Posts

Standard square posts without detail are baseline. Upgrades include:

- 115sq or 155sq posts (~$200 difference retail)
- Recesses (adds character, ~$120 extra)
- Decorative tops (~$75)

### Treads and Risers

Material choices significantly impact cost:

- **Carpet**: Cheapest option with MDF base
- **Laminate**: Similar to carpet in construction
- **Timber**: Maple (cheapest), Victorian Ash (mid-range), Oak ($1,500–$2,000 extra)
- **Painted**: Budget-friendly alternative with Hamptons/Provincial aesthetic appeal

## Design Styles

Three popular contemporary approaches:

1. **Standard**: Simple, functional design
2. **Hamptons**: Painted timber with traditional details
3. **Modern**: Open treads, glass balusters, minimal framing
