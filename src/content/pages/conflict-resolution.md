# Conflict Resolution During and After a New Home Build

## During Construction

### Independent Inspector

A third-party inspector can assess defects against building standards, offering objective analysis of whether issues comply with regulations or represent generally acceptable conditions.

### Building Surveyor

Either the homeowner or builder may assign a building surveyor. This selection remains the owner's choice.

### Home Owner Warranty Coverage

**Failure to Commence:** The Home Building Compensation Fund covers losses if construction doesn't begin, with claims available up to 12 months after failure to start work.

**Failure to Complete:** Homeowners can claim up to 20% of the contract value if the builder abandons work, claimable within 12 months of cessation.

## After Construction Completes

### Warranty Periods

**13 Weeks — Defects and Liability Period**

Minor issues like wall cracks or floorboard gaps fall under this initial coverage window.

**2 Years — Minor/Non-Structural Defects**

Most reasonable defects discovered in the home receive coverage during this timeframe.

**6 Years — Major/Structural Defects**

This covers significant structural problems affecting the building's stability, safety, or continued practical use.

### Important Actions

It's important to lodge a complaint if you are close to your 2 and 6 year warranty period to preserve protections and gain an additional six-month discretionary extension. Notify builders of formal complaints while remaining open to collaborative resolution.

### Mandatory Warranty Insurance

For projects exceeding $20,000, builders must provide homeowner warranty insurance through providers like iCare, covering situations where the builder dies, disappears, becomes insolvent, or ceases operations.
