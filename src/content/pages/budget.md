# Budget

There are a few things to consider when building a home.

## Base Price

This is the cost of building the same home on a level block and no changes made to the design

## Site Costs

<pre>Site Costs: 20-140k+</pre>

Even for a flat block with ideal conditions a builder will charge you for this. Standard site costs are minimal. They can be broken down further.

You may decide to have a bigger split in your home so your DEB cost is lower but your higher ceilings are more.

### Drop Edge Beams (DEB) (0-40k+)

Retaining walls positioned beneath the house structure.

### Splits in home (0-40k+)

Higher ceilings with associated increased expenses.

### Hydraulics (10-30k)

This is to manage stormwater for your home from the roof and hard surfaces. This wont be a fixed price from the builder as they need to pay engineers to design a system for your property and there are multiple options to solve the problem.

#### OSD

Onsite detention tank – This is a tank underground that retains water and slowly releases it to an outlet.

#### Charged System

These are where water from your pipes go to a rainwater tank and from there the excess water is fed uphill to a drain.

#### Silt arrestor pit

This is a gravel pit in your garden with a grate on top.

#### Spreader system

Gravel channel where water collects and spreads it across the channel.

#### Easement

This is where you need to allow water from your property to flow through an adjoining one. Neighbours rarely consent due to property value and usage impacts.

### Rock Removal (5-40k)

Unexpected rocks encountered during site cutting; requires breaking and removal.

### Soil Removal

Import or export costs when cutting reveals soil surplus or deficit.

### Traffic Management/Control (5-10k)

OH&S requirements depending on location. Builder typically requests provisional amount, refunding unused portions.

### Extra Piering

Applies to non-fixed-price site cost tenders.

## Inclusions

Builders have varying inclusions like 2740mm ceilings or polyurethane kitchen cabinets that others may exclude in their base and can add up quickly so never just compare the base price.

- 2740mm ceilings to both floors in a double storey
- Polyurethane kitchens
- 40mm stone benchtops
- 600x600mm tiles or laminate

## Owner Costs

### Demolition

<pre>Budget: $22-40k+ (Costs have gone up over 50% in 2022)</pre>

If you are doing a Knock Down Rebuild (KDR) then you will need to demolish the property thats there before the build can commence.

#### Demolition CDC

<pre>Budget: $880-1000</pre>

This is normally on top of the cost of the demolition cost and is fairly straight forward. Its valid for 5 years after approval.

#### Gas Abolishment

<pre>Budget: $1,141</pre>

Before demolishing your property you need to apply for a gas abolishment. In NSW, Jemena handles this within 20 business days (property must be vacant).

#### NBN & Electricity Disconnection

<pre>Budget: $300 for Electricity</pre>

If you have a smart meter then it can take a bit longer to disconnect your electricity. EnergyAustralia units disconnect faster than others.

### Council Contribution

<pre>Budget: 1% of build cost (single residential homes), $8,400-20,000</pre>

Aka Section 7.11/7.12 Environmental, or previously Section 94 Contribution Plan, or Planning & Assessment Act, 1979 (EP&A Act) contribution plans.

This varies from council to council and is payable by yourself to the council.

Check your 10.7 certificate to determine applicability.

Section 7.12 applies to single residential homes including knock down rebuilds.

Section 7.11 applies to a number of other types of properties including subdivisions, granny flats and duplexes (cost up to $20k).

### Driveway

<pre>Budget: $9-13k</pre>

For a standard coloured concrete double driveway this can cost around $11k.

#### Crossover Replacement

<pre>Budget: $4-5k</pre>

Many builders will include this cost in the tender so it's not normally a surprise.

### OHS (Occupational Health & Safety)

#### Tiger Tails

<pre>Budget: $2,000</pre>

These are installed at the demo stage of the build and remain until the build has completed. Electrical wire covers for truck visibility. Not always required; builders often don't mention until tender stage.

#### Engineered Pool Cover / Pool Void Cover / Pool Hard Cover

<pre>Budget: $2,400-4,000</pre>

These are normally required if you have a new or existing pool in your property or a permanent fence-enclosed pool. Hard cover allowing foot traffic while preventing falls. Verify builder requirements.

### Pool

<pre>Budget: Fibreglass: $50,000-$75,000
Concrete: $60,000-$100,000+</pre>

### Solar

<pre>Budget: Tesla Battery: $16,000
Solar Panels: $5,000-$25,000</pre>

Builders can offer solar as a package but this is just an offer from an affiliate of theirs and doesnt offer any major benefits unless they wont pre-wire the home for you. Its still possible to do this after handover but you may have pipes along the side of your home, though look further into this.

You may be able to get a government rebate to offset some of your costs. Its worth looking into a battery system like Teslas so that you dont pay for electricity and you can get paid for supplying power to the grid.

Its difficult to justify the ROI for solar batteries that cost upwards of $10k. Tesla batteries have a 10-year warranty with approximately a 13-year payback period.

### General Items

These can be done after the build but may be required to get a full occupancy certificate. For an occupancy certificate you will need at least:

- Letterbox
- Clothing line

### Retaining Walls

If your property is lower than your neighbours or if there is a step in the land you will need to retain the soil. Below a certain height you can do this without involving an engineer and once above it you would need to engage one to design an appropriate one. This can become very costly to do. You may be able to do this yourself and where required have it certified by an engineer.

A retaining wall will need to start at a certain level below the surface and in some cases come further out away from the soil to retain to form an L-shape. Options include treated timber, timber look concrete sleepers, and sandstone.

### Fencing

For properties built on new land you may not have a fence around the perimeter of the property. The cost can be shared between yourself and your neighbour. Once you get a few quotes you can share these with the neighbour and come to a decision and they are required to pay for half but this may be for the cheapest option if they choose. Also consider a front fence that would be more expensive.

### Landscaping

After your build the land around the house will not be level and may even slope towards the house. The cost of landscaping can be considerable even for blocks of land that are 400-500 sqm. Quotes can easily come to over $100k for a low to medium feature design.

- Front fencing: $12,000+
- Levelling, soil and turf: $15,000-25,000
- Landscape designer: $800+
- Adding a concrete perimeter to the house which slopes away from the house
- Levelling the land, importing soil underlay and turf
- Plants

### Steps from the house or alfresco

Unless otherwise requested, steps from the alfresco or the house eg. laundry, will need to be done after the build. These can be built by concrete or timber and there are cost effective ways of achieving this. You might be able to do this yourself as bunnings sells timber stair frames or you can construct it yourself. Over a certain height you may require balustrades. It can also form part of a deck.

### Independent Building Inspector

<pre>Budget: $800-4,000</pre>

There are different stages that an inspector is normally involved in which you can instruct them to inspect the build. Its a service you pay for where you are the client. Each stage can cost up to about $800-900 per inspection. More information can be found in [Construction Phases](/construction-phases/).

### Arborist Report

<pre>Budget: +-$1,500</pre>

If there are any trees that meet a threshold you may require an arborist to write a report that includes things like the zone of interference. These would be for trees that dont require removal but may require tree protection.

### Acoustic Report

<pre>Budget: $1,650-$3,800</pre>

If your property is impacted by noise like aircrafts overhead or a trainline you may need to get a report that will assess the impact to your property. This will affect the materials used in your build eg. double glazing, wall insulation, etc. You can find out if you are impacted by checking the 10.7 certificate for your property or if you live near a railway you may require this without there being a mention on the 10.7 certificate.

### BAL Report

If your property is in a bushfire zone you will need to get a BAL (Bushfire Attack Level) rating to determine what materials you need to use to protect your property.

Materials you may need to use for instance are aluminium flyscreens, solid treated timber front door, etc. You can find out if you are impacted by checking the 10.7 certificate for your property.
