# Checking Your House Plans & How to Correctly Read Them

If it's not on the plans or at least in your tender then it won't be done. Carefully checking your plans before signing off can save you from costly surprises.

## Key Internal Elements to Verify

### Room Dimensions

Check that room sizes match your expectations and account for wall thickness (typically 70–90mm internal walls, 240mm external walls). Dimensions can cascade — extending one room may require reducing another.

### Windows

Examine several critical aspects:

- Size codes (which vary by manufacturer like Wideline vs. Stegbar)
- Head heights relative to ceiling and eaves
- Exact positioning and centreline locations
- Opening directions and type (sliding, casement, etc.)
- Sill heights, especially for floor-to-ceiling installations
- Skylight placement relative to roof pitch

### Doors

Verify door widths (typically 720mm), opening directions, and type (hinged, cavity, barn). Ensure wardrobes and ensuites haven't been accidentally omitted.

### Bathrooms

Key checkpoints include floor waste location, shower entrance clearance, tap and showerhead positioning, vanity sizing, and shower niche dimensions (standard around 600mm wide).

### Other Interior Features

- Coffered ceiling dimensions and wall positioning
- Bulkhead heights and door provisions
- Air conditioning duct routing (approximately 300–400mm diameter)
- Electrical outlet placement

## External Considerations

### Site Planning

Verify setbacks match council requirements and floor level transitions. Properties with slope variations may require split-level designs affecting costs and aesthetics.

### Utility Placement

Check locations for rainwater tanks, hot water systems, and air conditioning units relative to windows and outdoor spaces. Ideally, hot water systems sit near plumbing fixtures.

### Materials & Drainage

Confirm wall materials (brick, render, Hebel, weatherboard) and roof specifications. Examine downpipe locations — sometimes repositioning them between corners is negotiable.

## Professional Tip

Request .dwg files from your builder to overlay and compare plans in detail, or trace PDFs using tools like floorplanner.com for detailed verification.
