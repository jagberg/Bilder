# Steel or Timber Frame

The choice between steel and timber frames for home construction depends on personal preference. Both materials have distinct advantages and disadvantages.

## Comparison Table

| Factor | Timber | Steel |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Termite protection** | Susceptible to termites; barrier installed for protection | Pest-free material |
| **Hanging Items** | Standard screws work easily | Requires special screws and drilling |
| **Straight walls** | May bow depending on tradesperson skill | Consistently straight walls |
| **Noise** | Not an issue | Some report expansion/contraction sounds |
| **Cost** | Similar to steel | Similar to timber |
| **Fire Resistance** | Susceptible; can be treated | Better heat retention than timber |
| **Assembly** | Built on-site to measure | Pre-built to exact specifications |
| **Future Modifications** | Easy to remove and add materials | Difficult to modify or extend |
| **Thermal Effect** | Doesn't conduct heat; no transfer through frame | Conducts heat/cold; requires better insulation; NCC 2022 thermal bridging requirements may increase costs |
| **Mould Risk** | Lower condensation risk | Higher mould risk due to condensation and thermal bridging |
| **Sustainability** | More sustainable material with less embodied energy | More carbon-intensive production |
| **Notable Brands** | — | TRUECORE steel commonly used |

## Key Considerations

Both materials offer comparable pricing. Timber provides better sustainability credentials and modification flexibility, while steel delivers superior pest resistance and dimensional consistency.
