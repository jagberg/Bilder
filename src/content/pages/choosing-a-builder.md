# Choosing a New Home Builder

Once you know your budget you will need to choose a new home builder that can build your home in your price bracket — but take into consideration the types of homes they build, quality, speed, communication, and more.

## Different Types of New Home Builds

There are different types of builders generally classified into 4 groups:

- Project/volume builder who has a number of floorplans and facades to choose from
- Project/volume builder who offers the above as well as full custom builds
- Custom builder who builds according to newly designed plans
- Owner builder — people who generally have experience in the industry and manage the build themselves

### Project/Volume Builder

**Advantages:**

- Multiple display homes available to view (caution: they include lots of expensive upgrades)
- Modifications to the floorplans are generally possible — most of the time anything except for the staircase can be altered
- Manages everything from start to finish including CDC or DA

**Disadvantages:**

- Build 100s to 1,000s of properties per year
- High attrition rate between sales reps, Customer Service Managers (CSM) and Site Supervisors
- Lengthy admin times — don't trust their timelines, get feedback from others building with them
- Build quality is hit and miss
- Franchise models (Adenbrook, GJ Gardner, Hotondo) mean each franchise can perform very differently

### Project/Volume Builder (Custom)

**Advantages:**

- Choice of floorplans and facades
- Can build off bespoke plans
- Offer a design service through a building designer (cost typically up to $5,000)
- Alternatively, bring your own designs — the builder can tender off these and continue to CDC or DA approval

**Build Volume & Quality:**

- Builds 10s to 100s of properties per year
- Attrition rates aren't as high for Sales reps, CSM and Site Supervisors
- Build quality is generally good

### Custom Builder

- Builds off bespoke plans
- Builds 15–20 properties per year, some much less
- Site supervisors don't manage more than 10 builds at a time — quality is easier to manage

### Owner Builder

- Builds off bespoke plans and manages the build themselves
- Finance is unlikely and normally requires self-funding
- Savings from being hands on
- May not get the same pricing discounts as larger builders

## Factors to Consider Between Each Group

### Initial Research

- Floorplans on offer
- Facades available
- What modifications can be made
- Inclusions on offer

### Initial Screening

- Online presence (reviews, social posts)
- Communication
- The types of builds they do (volume or custom)
- Pricing
- How long they have existed

### Secondary Screening

- How many homes they build per year
- How many homes their supervisors manage
- Staff turnover
- Standard inclusions
- Infringements (e.g. NCAT judgements)
- Building licence
- Building inspector feedback

## Builders Licence Check

Always check a builder's licence as it can identify serious issues with the business. In NSW you can find this on Verify Licence. Key things to check:

- Is the licence active or suspended? 🚩 Red flag if suspended
- How long have they been in business? 🚩 Red flag if recent — sometimes done to get insurance for another entity
- History of issues:
  - 🚩 Licence suspended
  - 🚩 Tribunal order — builder forced to rectify a problem through a court proceeding
  - 🚩 Licence condition — means the builder is not allowed to build houses

## Credit Checks

Resources to check the financial risk of your builder:

- **CreditWatch** — A free trial is offered, but well worth paying for. Pay particular attention to risk data of defaults, court actions and regulatory notices. Risk scores are alphabetically descending where A is the least risk.
- **Dun & Bradstreet** — Similar to CreditWatch
- Builder's accountant — If the builder is willing, ask to speak with their accountant to understand any financial risks

## Online Reviews

### ProductReview

Verified reviews, but may not provide a good cross section of feedback:

- **Positive reviews** could be solicited from people who had a good experience or who have low expectations
- **Negative reviews** — clients mid-build won't post negative reviews as the builder can retaliate

### Google Reviews

Not often used; many reviews aren't that useful.

### Facebook Groups

Gives the most accurate view of people building with a certain builder:

- Building New Homes In Sydney and NSW
- Metricon New Build Discussion Group

## References for the Builder

### Individuals Who Used the Builder

Ask the builder for about 5 or more references from clients at various stages:

- Currently in selections process
- In the middle of building
- Completed their build recently
- Completed their build a few years ago

**Questions to ask:**

- What are you building — custom or modified?
- Why did you choose them?
- How did you feel about their contract and the deposit required?
- How accurate have the Provisional Allowances been vs the real prices?
- Have you had variations after the deposit?
- What significant changes did you make after the tender deposit?
- What issues did you have with drafting?
- Have you had any issues since signing?
- How quickly did they deal with issues?
- What have their communications been like?
- How was your experience with the selections process?
- What would you advise me to look out for in general?
- Do you feel they have been honest in their dealings with you?
- Did you use an inspector — what did they say about quality?
- What maintenance issues have you had?

### Building Inspectors

Call a few different building inspectors to find out if they have inspected homes from the builder before. They will often have good insights into the quality of construction.
