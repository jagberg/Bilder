# Demolishing a House

## What to Consider When Choosing a Demo Company

When selecting a demolition contractor, evaluate:

- **Asbestos handling**: Confirm whether they clean and separate asbestos materials, as improper disposal significantly increases costs
- **Coverage scope**: Understand what asbestos scenarios they address, including discoveries beneath the structure
- **Client references**: Seek independent referrals through Facebook groups rather than relying on Google reviews, as many homeowners encounter unexpected cost variations of $8,000–$15,000+
- **Company history**: Verify how long they've operated and whether previous entities entered liquidation

## Preparing for Demolition

### CDC Application

- **Cost**: $935
- **Validity**: 5 years
- **Timeline**: Approximately 3 weeks (includes neighbour notification within 20 metres)

Specify demolition scope carefully; estimates exceeding $25,000 move into higher council fee brackets.

### Service Disconnections Required

**Gas (via Jemena in NSW)**

- Timeline: 4–6 weeks
- Cost: $1,151.70
- Requirement: "Vacant property" status mandatory

**Electrical**

- Timeline: 1 week
- Cost: $385
- Process: Level 2 electrician disconnects power cables; smart metres may process faster

**NBN**

- Timeline: 1 week
- Cost: None for demolition cases

## Salvageable Items

You cannot sell your flooring in some cases — when the walls are demolished they fold onto the floors. Consider selling windows, doors, kitchen cabinets, air conditioning units, and garage doors instead.
