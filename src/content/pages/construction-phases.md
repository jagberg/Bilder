# Construction Phases for a New Build Home

Construction for residential homes typically follows five main phases, though specific steps vary based on materials and techniques used.

## Five Main Construction Phases

1. **Site & Foundation** — Demolition or site levelling, followed by foundation work including footings, drainage, moisture barriers, and termite protection

2. **Frame** — Construction of frames and external walls with render, roof installation, gutters, cladding, and insulation. Electricians and plumbers install essential utilities

3. **Lockup Stage** — Installation of all doors and windows, making the structure secure and weatherproof so trades can work safely indoors

4. **Fitout** — Installation of electrical fixtures, lights, powerpoints, kitchen/bathroom/laundry cabinets, benchtops, tiling, tapware, mirrors, and bathroom fixtures

5. **Practical Completion** — Final painting, installations, and detailing before handover (pending any required rectifications)

## Slab Stage (Detailed Breakdown)

### Key Steps

- **Peg out survey** — Stakes mark residential boundaries and key level points
- **Sediment control** — Barriers prevent soil/debris runoff
- **Excavation and site preparation** — Land shaped to proper levels for compacted base
- **Concrete piers** — Drilled supports approximately 400mm diameter at engineered intervals
- **Slab formwork** — Perimeter outline built to retain concrete
- **Internal drainage and termite collars** — Plumbing installed before concrete pour
- **BIAX/waffle pods and steel** — Modern suspended slab systems with structural reinforcement
- **Concrete pour** — Typical residential strength: 20–40 MPa, finished with power trowels

## Required Inspections

NSW Fair Trading and structural engineers must inspect multiple stages:

- After commencement of excavation for, and before placement of, footings
- Before pouring reinforced concrete elements
- Before framework coverage
- Prior to waterproofing coverage in wet areas
- Before stormwater drainage connection coverage
- After completion, before occupation

Plumbing inspections are mandatory for internal/external drainage, recycled water systems, and final fixtures.

## Homeowner Checkpoints

Owners can verify pier counts and locations to ensure engineering specifications were followed on-site.
