# Pre-Construction Phase

The pre-construction phase covers everything that happens between signing your contract and breaking ground. Getting this phase right sets the foundation for a smooth build.

## Key Activities in Pre-Construction

### Design Finalisation

Working with your builder or architect to finalise all plans, inclusions, and specifications before construction begins. Changes become increasingly expensive once construction starts.

### Approvals

Obtaining the necessary approvals (CDC or DA) is typically the longest part of pre-construction:

- **CDC**: 2–4 weeks
- **DA**: 2 months to 12+ months depending on council and complexity

### Engineering

Structural engineering, geotechnical reports, and energy assessments (BASIX/NatHERS) are completed during this phase.

### Selections

Choosing all inclusions — kitchen, bathrooms, flooring, tiles, lighting, and more. This process can take several months with a volume builder.

## What to Watch For

- **Price lock-in expiry**: Your fixed price may expire if pre-construction takes too long
- **Variation costs**: Any changes to plans after signing attract significant additional charges
- **Delays**: Admin and approval delays are common — don't rely on builder timeline estimates without independent verification

## Next Steps

Once approvals are in place and selections are finalised, demolition (if required) can proceed followed by construction.
