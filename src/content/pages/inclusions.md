# Builder Inclusions and Selection Options for a New Build

Builders offer varying inclusion packages that can add $25,000–$100,000 to a home's cost. Review individual inclusions rather than accepting package deals, as bundled options don't provide credits for unwanted features.

## Structural Upgrades

### Ceiling Height

**Cost: $5,000–$9,500 per floor**

Standard ceilings range from 2.4m to 2.55m. Upgrading to 2.74m is popular and enhances resale value. Local regulations (CDC typically 8.5m, DA potentially 9m+) may restrict maximum heights.

### Windows & Doors

- Standard doors measure 2040mm × 820mm; increasing ceiling height justifies upgrading to 2340mm
- Hollow core doors are standard; solid doors cost significantly more
- Options include cavity sliders (space-saving) and external doors with storm protection
- Wheelchair accessibility requires minimum 850mm width

### Roof Options

- **Colorbond**: Consider Anticon foil-based insulation; some report hearing rain noise
- **Tiled**: May require structural changes with steel frames (approximately $3,500 extra)

### Recessed Flooring & Doors

- **Ground floor wet areas**: Up to $1,000
- **Upper floor bathrooms**: $1,000–$2,800 per bathroom
- **Recessed stacker doors**: $350–$900 per set

## Internal Upgrades

### Air Conditioning

**Cost: $0–$30,000**

Options include:

- Split systems vs. ducted units
- Multiple zones (Actron allows 2–8 zones; upgrading costs $350–$500 per zone initially)
- Duct types: round, multi-directional, or linear/slimline ($240–$380 per location)

### Flooring

- **Tiles**: Larger formats (600×600mm) add labour costs up to $40/m²
- **Laminate**: Upgrading from carpet costs approximately $5,000–$10,000 upstairs
- **Acoustic underlay**: Improves soundproofing with AAAC rating (4–6 stars)
- **Waterproofing**: Hybrid or vinyl preferred; modern laminate systems are effective

### Electrical Features

- Downlights: $120–$140 each or $75 to provision
- Home automation options available
- Outdoor and garden lighting upgrades

### Ceilings, Cornices & Skirting

- **Square set ceilings**: Approximately $2,000
- **Standard skirting**: 66mm height; upgraded options reach 90mm or larger
- **Square set windows/doors**: $150–$200 per opening

### Soundproofing

**Between floors:**

- Soundscreen insulation: $2,000–$6,500
- Hebel PowerFloors: $20,000–$35,000
- Fyrchek/Soundchek: $3,000–$7,000
- Sound isolation clips: $1,800 per room

**Between rooms:**

- Insulation: $2,000–$5,500
- Dense gyprock walls: $3,500–$7,000

### Additional Features

- **Laundry chute**: $2,000
- **Solar panels**: $6,000–$25,000; ROI typically 13+ years
- **Heat pump hot water**: $2,000–$6,000 (cheaper to operate than gas)
- **Coffered ceilings**: $2,000–$4,000

## External Upgrades

- Outdoor taps (all corners): $120 per tap
- Gas to alfresco: $275
- Gas to pool: $275
- First floor balcony taps: $500
- Dog doors: Custom pricing (requires structural consideration)

## Key Takeaway

It's important to spend time going through what inclusions you want in your build first and then pricing these individually with the builder. This approach allows better assessment of whether package deals provide genuine value.
