# Knowing What You Can Build

Before designing your home, you need to understand what you're legally permitted to build on your block. In NSW, there are two primary pathways: **CDC (Complying Development Certificate)** and **DA (Development Application)**.

## Key Development Options

### CDC vs DA

- **CDC**: More restrictive; requires full compliance with regulations. Faster processing (2–4 weeks)
- **DA**: Council-specific requirements; more flexible but longer processing times (days to months)

## Critical Planning Considerations

### Setbacks

These are distance requirements from property boundaries:

- **Side setbacks**: Ground floor typically 0.9m to 1.5m from neighbours; upper floors set further back
- **Front setbacks**: Varies by location — either averaging neighbouring properties or following specific measurements (5.5m to garage line, 4.5m to building facade)

### Floor Space Ratio (FSR)

Calculates usable floor area by accounting for:

- Total house area minus garage space, staircase, voids, alfresco, and porch
- CDC generally allows up to 50% less than DA

### Building Height

- **CDC**: Maximum 8.5m
- **DA**: Slightly higher, council-dependent
- Measured from ground level to roof peak

## Research Resources

The article recommends consulting:

- Council websites (e.g., Georges River Council mapping tools)
- NSW Spatial Portal for topographic data
- DCP (Development Control Plan) and LEP (Local Environment Plan) documents
- AllHomes website for property dimensions

**Essential first step**: Call your council to ask what the limitations are on your specific block.
