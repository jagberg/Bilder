# Common Mistakes in a Build Journey

## Contracts and Negotiating Changes

Timing is critical when negotiating with builders. The best time to negotiate terms is before you pay the builder any significant amount of money and you have the option of moving on.

Key recommendations:

- Request all contracts (Works Agreement, HIA contract) before signing
- Have legal review completed upfront
- Recognise that builders are unlikely to modify terms favourably after receiving 5–10% deposits
- Avoid becoming locked into unfavourable conditions once substantial payments are made

## Timelines and Builder Mistakes

Expectations should be realistic regarding construction delays. Most volume builders operate similarly — delays and communication gaps are standard across the industry unless you're investing in premium bespoke construction.

Important considerations:

- Smaller builders may offer better service but typically charge higher rates
- Higher costs reflect smaller operations unable to spread administrative expenses across multiple projects
- Verify timeline estimates by speaking directly with other clients
- Judge builders on how they resolve problems rather than expecting perfection

## Planning Stage Mistakes

### Architect Plans and Copyright Issues

Unless it's explicitly stated or you've paid for it, the plans belong to the architect. Architects typically retain copyright unless contractual arrangements specify otherwise.

Consequences of copyright disputes:

- Builders may refuse plan usage entirely
- Fees to release copyright range from $1,000–$5,500
- Without .dwg files, new draftsmen must trace PDFs or work from dimensions, risking omissions

### Obtaining Builder Input Before Approvals

Securing builder cost estimates before applying for DA or CDC prevents:

- Costly redesigns if budgets are exceeded post-approval
- Six-month approval delays that coincide with material price increases
- Incompatibilities between architect designs and builder construction methods

### Site-Specific Costs

**Hydraulics:** Sloped properties require stormwater management solutions. Water runoff control through street charging, onsite containment, or neighbour easements adds significant expense.

**Rock Removal:** Unpredictable costs since borehole testing cannot fully assess subsurface conditions. Specialised machinery and removal services create variable expenses.

## Additional Cost Areas

**Landscaping:** Simple turf installation ranges from $20,000–$50,000 depending on site access and conditions. Retaining walls require engineering reports and carry substantial expenses.

**Owner-Provided Items:** Plans often designate certain elements as owner responsibility, including retaining walls, alfresco steps, and environmental reports (arborist/noise assessments).
