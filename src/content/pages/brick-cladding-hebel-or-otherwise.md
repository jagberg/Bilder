# Brick, Cladding, Hebel or Otherwise

When selecting external wall materials for a new home build, evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of brick veneer, rendered surfaces, Hebel panels, and cladding options. Each material presents distinct considerations regarding aesthetics, maintenance, thermal performance, and cost.

## Brick Veneer/Facebrick

Brick veneer consists of a single course of decorative bricks applied over a timber or steel frame structure.

**Advantages:**

- Minimal maintenance requirements
- No repainting necessary to maintain appearance
- Durable long-term finish

**Disadvantages:**

- Premium pricing for quality facebricks
- Requires skilled labour for proper installation
- Chipped edges reveal reddish base colour underneath
- Visible discolouration may occur on parapet walls

**Cost Considerations:**

Standard ranges typically cost around $1,000 per 1,000 bricks through builder discounts. Premium options like reclaimed varieties can exceed $3,000 per 1,000 bricks, potentially adding $5,000–$45,000 for double-storey homes.

## Rendered Options

### Rendered Common Bricks

Economical extruded bricks combined with acrylic render create an affordable rendered appearance, though render application costs approximately $25,000 for entire house coverage.

### Hebel PowerPanel

Hebel is a lightweight aerated concrete that has been around for over 50 years and was invented in Germany. It offers:

- Similar cost to brick veneer
- Quick installation process
- Improved thermal performance (nearly 3× better than brick)
- Better acoustic properties
- Potential chipping, cracking, and difficulty hanging heavy items

### EPS (Expanded Polystyrene)

This lightweight material requires acrylic render finishing. Important note: **EPS is banned from use in certain types of buildings in Victoria and it may impact house insurance costs.**

### Blueboard/James Hardie Fine Texture

Pre-sealed fibre cement boards eliminate rendering requirements, reducing installation time and cost.

## Cladding Systems

Cladding materials install quickly with minimal mess and require only painting rather than rendering. However, they offer negligible thermal or acoustic benefits. Options include:

- **James Hardie Scyon:** Overlapping fibre cement boards
- **James Hardie Matrix:** Non-overlapping panels requiring joint sealant

All cladding requires house wrap for moisture management and draft prevention.

## Thermal and Acoustic Performance

| Material | Thermal Rating | Acoustic Rating | Cost (p/m²) |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| NRG Greenboard (100mm) | R2.60–R3.31 | 39 Rw | $19–25 |
| Hebel PowerPanel (100mm) | R0.52 | 34 Rw | $50–60 |

Hebel provides substantially better thermal benefits than cladding but lower thermal value than insulation boards.

## Maintenance Requirements

**Bricks:** Require minimal maintenance beyond occasional cleaning; no repainting necessary.

**Render:** Requires repainting every several years; cracks need patching at potentially significant cost.

**Cladding:** Requires regular repainting; thermal expansion can cause gaps needing periodic caulking.
