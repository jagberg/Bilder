# Architect Plans – What You Get and the Important Parts

When building a custom home, you'll need to engage an architect or draftsman. An architect will normally design a functional home from the ground up, whereas a draftsman would take your sketches and professionally draw them up.

Avoid using builder design services, as this locks your design exclusively to that builder and prevents shopping it around to other contractors.

## Required Documentation

Before engaging an architect, provide:

- Survey plan (ideally with .dwg files)
- 10.7 certificate containing property information like BAL ratings and council regulations

## Two Design Stages

### Concept Design

This initial phase typically includes:

- Floorplan
- Facade and elevations
- Site plan
- Materials specification
- Optional extras: window/door schedules, landscape plans, 3D renderings

**Cost Range:** Nothing to $5,000+

### Working Drawings

This advanced stage covers:

- BASIX and NatHERS energy assessments
- Detailed roof and site plans
- Detailed elevations and sections

## Critical Ownership Issues

**Copyright & .dwg Files:** Ensure your contract explicitly grants you copyright ownership and includes digital .dwg files. Without these, you cannot modify designs or transfer them to other builders.

Design modifications must exceed 30% difference to be considered new work.

## Key Considerations

- Request clarification on revision limits upfront
- Compare architect costs against builder design services
- Get construction quotes before proceeding to working drawings
- Consider builder input for cost-efficient design modifications
