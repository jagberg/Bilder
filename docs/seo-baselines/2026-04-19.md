## Commentary / analysis

### GA trend: engagement improving
28-day engagement (30.4%) is up vs 90-day (26.7%). Avg session (102.5s) is longer than the 90-day figure (77.9s). Content-quality direction is correct.

### Homepage is a dead-end
- 161 sessions land on `/`, **95% bounce**, 1.07 pages/session, only 31s avg.
- Of the ~8 sessions that click through, next pages are spread across 20+ paths with no single page dominating. No functioning funnel.
- When content pages are entered directly (from Google), bounce drops dramatically:
  - `/architect-plans/` — 47.2% bounce, 155s avg
  - `/brick-cladding-hebel-or-otherwise/` — 50% bounce, 128s avg
  - `/construction-phases/` — 47.1% bounce, **482s avg**
- Implication: Google traffic works. Homepage doesn't guide visitors to it.

### Direct traffic looks like bot traffic
- 378 "direct" sessions (36% of total), **8.7% engagement rate**, 353 unique users.
- 83 separate sessions with `(not set)` landing page, 100% bounce, 0s duration — pure junk.
- Recommend Cloudflare bot filter + GA exclusion. Untreated, these numbers inflate "users" but depress engagement metrics.

### Bimodal deep-engagement pages
Small audience, massive dwell:
- `/budget/` — 8 sessions, **4.38 pages/sess**, 362s avg, 87.5% bounce
- `/lighting-tips-and-ideas-for-a-new-home/` — 8 sessions, **3.5 pages/sess**, **794s avg**, 62.5% bounce
- `/conflict-resolution/` — 8 views, **987s avg**

People who engage, engage hard. Worth surfacing internal links TO these pages from higher-traffic ones.

### AI referrers are appearing
ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Ahrefs all showing up. Small volume but 100% engagement. Worth tracking trend over time as AI search/discovery grows.

### Bing outperforms Google per-session
- Google organic: 523 sessions, 43.8% engaged
- Bing organic: 30 sessions, **50.0% engaged**

Small sample but consistent with 90-day data (Bing 54.7% vs Google 43.6%). Bing users are more qualified.

### Search Console: the snippet crisis

Pages rank in positions 1–3 for dominant queries but get 0.2–0.6% CTR. Normal CTR at those positions is 8–20%. Page-level breakdown:

**`/architect-plans/` (18,595 impressions, 50 clicks, 0.3% CTR)**
Ranks at position 1.5–3 for every variant of "architect plans", "architectural plans", "architecture plan". `architectural plan` alone: 6,383 impressions at position 1.6, 0.4% CTR (27 clicks). Meta title and description don't sell the click — Google is probably rewriting the title because the current one is uninformative.

If we can get to 4% CTR at similar positions (still below typical), that's ~740 clicks/90d vs current 50 — **~15x uplift** from title/description alone.

The `plans` query is a different story: 17,936 impressions, 7 clicks. Very generic intent (could be architect plans, floor plans, meal plans). Not worth over-optimizing for.

**`/brick-cladding-hebel-or-otherwise/` (8,810 impressions, 55 clicks, 0.6% CTR)**
Ranks position 1.6 for `hebel veneer` (1 click / 74 impr) and page 1 for most hebel-related variants. But `brick cladding` (867 impr) and `brick cladding australia` (719 impr) sit at positions 36–45 — off page 4. The title "Brick, Cladding, Hebel or Otherwise" is too vague for Google to match "brick cladding" strongly.

**`/windows/` (1,614 impressions, 3 clicks, 0.2% CTR, avg pos 38.2)**
Basically invisible. Queries are overwhelmingly pricing-intent (aluminium window price, bifold cost, cedar windows). The page has pricing data but it's buried ~80% down a 10k-word guide. Google can't extract it as a snippet. Current H1 "New Home Windows" doesn't match commercial intent.

Key queries and where they rank:
- `aluminium windows price` — 47 impr, pos 50 (page 5)
- `cedar windows` — 49 impr, pos 33 (page 3)
- `aluminium windows cost` — 13 impr, pos 76 (page 7)

### Competitive landscape for "aluminium windows price australia"

Top SERP (searched 2026-04-19):
1. **imlang.com.au** — "How Much Do Aluminium Windows Cost in Sydney & Melbourne? (2026 Guide)"
2. **aandd.au** — "Aluminium Window Prices in Australia: 7 Key Factors"
3. **clearstyle.com.au** — city-targeted pricing page
4. **yourhome.gov.au**, **energy.gov.au** — government, dominate informational intent
5. **jasonwindows.com.au** — manufacturer with dedicated price pages

Common patterns in what ranks:
- Year in title (2026 Guide)
- City / state targeting (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth)
- Listicle or question format
- Dedicated pricing URL (not pricing buried inside a guide)
- Explicit price ranges in snippets (e.g. "$300–$1,250 per square metre")
- FAQ / structured data

What our `/windows/` page is missing:
| Competitor does | `/windows/` does |
|---|---|
| Question/guide year in title | Generic H1 "New Home Windows" |
| Prices dated to current year | Pricing dated "Jan 22" (4+ years stale) |
| Dedicated pricing URL | Pricing buried 80% down a long guide |
| City/state in title | No geographic signal |
| FAQ / HowMuch schema | No structured data |
| Short scannable sections | Long dense prose blocks |
